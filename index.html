Buying a home is one of the single largest investments a person will make in their lifetime, and getting a home inspection is one of the best ways to help protect this investment. A pre-purchase home inspection is designed to highlight the positive aspects of the property as well as point out those features that may need attention.

During your inspection, I'll evaluate the property's current condition and estimate repairs and probable short-term expenses. At the end of the inspection, I'll hand you a thorough, hand-written report detailing everything that was found. Later on, I'll follow up with pictures and a summarized, typed report highlighting any notable deficiencies.

A comprehensive report

My report will detail both the positive and deficient aspects of the home's visible and accessible components. This includes:

  • structural condition and basement/crawl space
  • electrical, plumbing, hot water heater, heating and air conditioning
  • quality, condition and live expectancy of major systems
  • kitchen and appliances
  • general interior, including ceilings, walls, floors, windows, insulation and ventilation
  • general exterior, including roof, gutter, chimney, drainage and grading
  • estimates on repairs and probable expenses for the next three years
